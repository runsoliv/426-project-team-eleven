Project Description:

Our system simulates an emergency weather alert network that coordinates official severe weather warnings, community incident reports, and location-based emergency information during hurricanes, 
tornadoes, and floods. A single server may struggle with the surge in alerts, reports, and user requests during a large-scale emergency, when thousands of residents need accurate information at the same time.

Two regions illustrate who is most exposed. North Texas, especially the Dallas–Fort Worth area, faces frequent tornado threats, and residents of manufactured and mobile home communities 
may have very little time to respond. South Florida, especially the Miami–Fort Lauderdale area, faces hurricanes and severe flooding, with low-lying coastal neighborhoods and residents without reliable transportation among the most vulnerable. In both regions, delayed alerts or inaccurate reports can reduce the time people have to avoid dangerous areas. Our system combines official weather alerts with local reports about flooding, blocked roads, power outages, and other hazards so residents and emergency responders can receive relevant information for each area.
